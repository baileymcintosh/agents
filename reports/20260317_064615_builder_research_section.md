#### A1. CHRONOLOGY OF OPERATIONS

**The Pre-War Context (January – February 27, 2026)**

The conflict did not emerge from vacuum. A precise understanding of the pre-war period is essential to assessing both actors' intentions:

- **December 2025:** Nationwide Iranian protests over economic conditions and political repression were violently suppressed by the IRGC. Trump issued unprecedented public warnings that US intervention was being considered — a significant departure from previous administrations. (*International Crisis Group, "Finding an Off-Ramp," March 2026*)
- **January–February 2026:** The US executed the largest regional military buildup since the 2003 Iraq War, deploying two carrier battle groups and pre-positioning heavy bombers within striking distance of Iran.
- **February 6:** First round of US-Iran indirect talks in Muscat, Oman. Mediated by Omani FM Badr bin Hamad Al Busaidi. Delegations led by Iranian FM Abbas Araghchi on one side; Steve Witkoff and Jared Kushner on the other. Rubio publicly stated: *"I'm not sure you can reach a deal with these guys."* (*Wikipedia: 2025-2026 Iran–US Negotiations*)
- **February 17:** Second round in Geneva. Araghchi said "good progress." White House said the two sides remain "very far apart." Trump stated a 10-day decision window on February 19.
- **February 26:** Third round of talks in Geneva; Oman announced "significant progress" and said more talks would happen in Vienna the following week.
- **February 27:** Oman's FM announced Iran had agreed to degrade nuclear stockpiles to "the lowest level possible." Trump said he preferred diplomacy but all options remain open.
- **February 27, 3:38 PM EST:** Trump, traveling on Air Force One to Corpus Christi, Texas, gave the order to proceed with Operation Epic Fury. (*Wikipedia: 2026 Iran War*) This was one day after Iran appeared to concede on stockpiles — a fact whose interpretation is contested (see B2 below).

**Day 1 — February 28, 2026: "Operation Epic Fury" Launches**

The opening strikes were the most concentrated US-Israeli joint air campaign since Operation Desert Storm in 1991. By any technical measure, the scale and precision were historically unprecedented:

- **Time:** Around 9:45 AM IRST (1:15 AM EST), US missiles, drones, and Israeli fighter jets began striking Iran. The attack opened during the first day of Ramadan — a deliberate psychological and symbolic escalation.
- **Scale:** In the first 12 hours, the US carried out **900 strikes** against Iranian targets. The IDF confirmed **200 Israeli jets** struck nearly **500 targets** in Iran in Day 1 alone. (*JINSA, Operations Epic Fury and Roaring Lion: 3/1/26 Update*)
- **Assets employed:** One-way attack drones, Tomahawk cruise missiles, F/A-18 Super Hornets, F-35s, and confirmed reports of **at least four B-2 Spirit stealth bombers** carrying 30,000-pound GBU-57 Massive Ordnance Penetrators (MOPs) — the only munition capable of threatening deeply buried facilities. (*JINSA, 3/1/26*)
- **Leadership decapitation — first 12 hours:** The strikes killed **at least 40 Iranian military and political leaders** in the opening barrage. Most critically, **Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei** was killed at his compound in Tehran, confirmed by both Trump and Netanyahu. Also killed: **IRGC Commander-in-Chief** (second such killing in less than one year, following June 2025), **Chief of Staff Ali Asghar Hejazi** (one of Khamenei's top candidates to succeed him), and an estimated 48 total senior Iranian leaders per Trump's Fox News statement. (*Time; Wikipedia; JINSA*)
- **CBS News** reported 40 officials killed, with uncertainty about whether they were co-located or in multiple locations — suggesting a compound strike of the kind used against Bin Laden but at scale.
- **Nuclear targets struck on Day 1:** Fordow, Natanz, and Isfahan hit with the heaviest available munitions (see A2 below for detailed assessment).
- **Iranian retaliation — Day 1:** Iran launched approximately **35 missiles** at Israel in the initial response, using Emad and Ghadr medium-range ballistic missiles, with reports of Kheybar Shekan or Fatah-1 use. Iran also attacked **14 US bases** across the region: Bahrain, Qatar, UAE, Kuwait, Jordan, Saudi Arabia. In the late evening of Day 1, 10 confirmed missile and drone hits across 3 countries caused **multiple fatalities, including at least 3 US service members.** (*JINSA 3/1/26; ISW Special Report Feb 28*) A follow-on barrage of 63 projectiles struck on March 1.

**Days 2–5 (March 1–4, 2026): Escalation Solidifies**

- **March 1:** Iran established an interim governing council under Article 111 of the Iranian Constitution: President Pezeshkian, Supreme Court Chief Justice Mohseni-Eje'i, and senior cleric Alireza Arafi. IRGC security chief Ali Larijani issued a statement warning "secessionist groups" against exploiting the crisis. (*Al Jazeera, March 1*)
- **Israel launches Operation Roaring Lion:** Parallel to Epic Fury, Israel initiated ground and air operations in southern Lebanon — "Operation Roaring Lion" — as Hezbollah opened a second front on March 2 (see A4).
- **Iranian strikes on Gulf states:** Iran launched barrages at UAE (~165 missiles by March 3), Kuwait (~97), Qatar (~65), Bahrain, and Saudi Arabia. Gulf countries were intercepting ~94% of incoming projectiles, per ISW reporting. However, the remainder caused infrastructure damage. Iranian drones struck desalination plants (UAE accused on March 8) and targeted oil refineries near Tehran in Israeli strikes the same day.
- **Day 3–4 (March 3–4):** IDF claimed the destruction of a "secret nuclear weapons development site" at what has been identified as the Parchin-Taleghan 2 complex — a site with a documented history of nuclear weapons research.
- **March 3:** Trump announced to supporters that Iran's conventional military was "essentially destroyed." White House published its first operational summary on March 3.
- **March 5:** White House summary: Congress reaffirmed Trump's constitutional authority. US missile launchers and drone-production facilities being systematically sealed. (*White House, March 5, 2026*)

**Days 6–10 (March 5–10, 2026): Systematic Degradation**

- **March 7–8:** Iran-Iraq War-style intensity. Forty-one people killed in Israeli strikes on Nabi Chit, eastern Lebanon; Israeli forces conducted a commando landing operation. Israel struck two oil refineries and two oil storage facilities near Tehran on March 8.
- **March 8 (critical milestone):** The **Assembly of Experts named Mojtaba Khamenei** as Iran's new Supreme Leader. (See B1 for full analysis.) Iranian state TV announced the appointment "early Monday" — approximately Day 9 of the conflict.
- **March 10 (Day 11):** US CENTCOM Commander Admiral Brad Cooper and Secretary Hegseth publicly committed to "the most intense day of strikes" yet — a 20% increase in strike sortie rate over any previous day. Satellite imagery confirmed strikes on missile bases at Baharestan and Khormuj, specifically targeting tunnel entrances to seal underground missile storage. The approach — physical denial of access rather than attempting deep penetration — reflects a shift in US targeting logic toward attrition of remaining stockpiles rather than destruction of infrastructure. (*The War Zone, March 10, 2026*)
- **Status by March 10:** An unspecified senior Israeli official told Israeli media that the combined US-Israel force had destroyed **80% of Iran's missile launchers** by this date. Iran's navy: 43 vessels destroyed or damaged, including confirmed sinking of IRIS Dena (torpedoed by US submarine off Sri Lanka) and disabling of IRIS Bushehr. **Iran's air defenses degraded by 80%.** Iran's airspace closed to most international traffic. (*JPost, March assessment; ISW March 10 Update*)
- **Naval dimension:** Iran's declared Hormuz closure triggered the largest commercial shipping rerouting since the Red Sea crisis of 2023. Diversions surged 360% from an average of 218/day pre-conflict to 1,010/day by March 10. All major carriers (Maersk, Hapag-Lloyd) suspended Mideast routes. (*Project44, March 10, 2026*)
