## 2. DETAILED ANALYSIS

### 2.1 The Opening Salvo — February 28, 2026

**Timeline of the first 48 hours:**

- **Pre-strike (hours before):** US Cyber Command and Space Command initiated operations to "disrupt, degrade, and blind" Iran's ability to communicate and respond. Air defense batteries checked systems; carrier strike groups moved to launch positions.
- **1:15 AM ET (0615 GMT / 9:45 AM Iran time), February 28:** More than 100 US and Israeli aircraft launched "forming a single synchronized wave." The daylight timing (against Israeli precedent of nighttime strikes) was deliberate to achieve tactical surprise. The first shots were US Navy Tomahawk missiles.
- **First hours:** The operation struck more than 1,000 targets in the first 24 hours, including IRGC command and control facilities, air defenses, missile and drone launch sites, military airfields, and the compound of Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei on Pasteur Street, Tehran.
- **Khamenei's death confirmed:** Trump announced on Truth Social that Khamenei had been killed. Iranian state media initially denied it ("steadfast and firm in commanding the field"), then confirmed his death on Sunday morning March 1, declaring 40 days of mourning. Israeli special forces and Mossad reportedly conducted a ground operation inside Iran on the same day (reported by Al Arabiya; not independently confirmed).
- **Other leadership targets:** Israel also reportedly targeted President Masoud Pezeshkian and former President Mahmoud Ahmadinejad; their condition remained unclear as of the initial reports.

**The operation was named:**
- US: **Operation Epic Fury** (CENTCOM, Admiral Brad Cooper commanding)
- Israel: **Operation Roaring Lion** (IDF, under PM Netanyahu and war cabinet)

**Key operational achievement:** By March 2, US Joint Chiefs of Staff Chairman General Dan Caine announced that "local air superiority has been established [over Iran]." B-1 bombers were deployed, followed by B-2 stealth bombers flying 37-hour round-trip sorties from the continental United States, demonstrating progressive degradation of Iranian air defenses.

**Stated US/Israeli objectives** (per Trump's video address, CENTCOM statements, and Pentagon briefings):
1. Overthrow/topple the Islamic Republic ("regime change")
2. Destroy Iran's missile capabilities
3. Destroy Iran's Navy
4. Ensure Iran never obtains a nuclear weapon
5. Dismantle the regime's internal security apparatus

### 2.3 Iranian Retaliation

Iran's response was rapid and multi-directional, demonstrating what analysts described as "a higher level of military preparedness" compared to the June 2025 war, suggesting pre-delegated response authorities:

**Against Israel:**
- Multiple waves of ballistic missiles and drones targeting Tel Aviv, Jerusalem, Beit Shemesh, and other cities
- Beit Shemesh strike (March 1): 8-9 killed, 20+ injured — the deadliest single strike on Israel
- Cluster munitions reportedly used against central Israel (March 16)
- March 16: Ballistic missile struck Jerusalem
- **Israeli casualties:** 15-17 killed, 3,138+ injured (as of March 15-16)

**Against Gulf States:**
- Iran fired approximately 2,000 projectiles at Gulf countries in the first two weeks
- **UAE:** Debris and missiles hit landmark buildings in Dubai and Abu Dhabi (St. Regis Hotel area); 5 civilians and 2 soldiers killed, 145 injured. Dubai flights suspended after drone attack near airport. UAE recalled ambassador to Israel.
- **Qatar:** Hamad International Airport targeted; 16 injured. Qatar shot down two Iranian Su-24 bombers on March 2 — first QEAF air-to-air kill ever. QatarEnergy declared force majeure on all LNG shipments March 4, removing 20% of global LNG supply overnight.
- **Kuwait:** Airport struck; 4 soldiers and 4 civilians killed, 99 injured. US Embassy compound hit. Kuwaiti air defenses accidentally shot down 3 US F-15E fighters in a friendly fire incident (March 2; all 6 crew ejected safely).
- **Saudi Arabia:** Riyadh and eastern region struck; 2 killed, 12 injured. Iran targeted major oil and gas infrastructure starting March 2.
- **Bahrain:** Residential building in Manama hit (March 10, 1 killed); US Navy 5th Fleet HQ targeted multiple times. 3 total killed, 38 injured.
- **Oman:** 3 killed; al Awhi Industrial Zone in Sohar struck by drone (March 13).
- **Jordan:** 19 injured.
- **Iraq:** PMF HQ in Baghdad struck (3 PMF fighters killed); US Embassy compound in Baghdad hit by missile. 27 total killed.
- **Azerbaijan:** Two Iranian drones struck Nakhchivan exclave (March 5), damaging airport. Azerbaijan withdrew diplomats.
- **Cyprus:** UK military bases on alert; at least one Iranian drone struck RAF facility.

**Strait of Hormuz closure:** The IRGC confirmed closure of the strait on March 2. As of March 13, at least 20 vessels had been affected by attacks (UKMTO), with 6-7 struck by projectiles or explosive-laden boats within a 48-hour window. Iran declared all US, Israeli, or allied tankers and commercial ships barred from passage. Over 150 tankers anchored outside rather than transit. Insurance coverage withdrawn or repriced. Maersk, MSC, CMA CGM, and Hapag-Lloyd suspended transits. ~1,000 ships carrying $25 billion in cargo stranded (Lloyd's Market Association).

## 4. IMPLICATIONS FOR THE OVERALL PROJECT

### For Scenario Analysis:
The conflict is now in its **third week** with no off-ramp visible. Both sides have rejected ceasefire mediation. The key variable is duration: every additional week of Hormuz closure compounds the economic damage nonlinearly. The scenarios should model:
- **Short war (4-6 weeks total):** US/Israel achieves military objectives, some form of ceasefire; Hormuz reopens. Probability: 25-30%
- **Extended air campaign (2-4 months):** No ground invasion but sustained bombing; Hormuz partially reopened with naval escorts. Probability: 35-40%
- **Broader regional war:** Gulf states drawn in militarily; Hezbollah full-scale war; possible ground operations in Iran. Probability: 15-20%
- **Regime collapse/change:** Internal uprising or IRGC coup during sustained bombing. Probability: 10-15%
- **Diplomatic resolution:** Requires face-saving off-ramp for both sides; very difficult given stated objectives. Probability: 5-10% near-term

### For Financial Markets Analysis:
The critical finding is the **two-chokepoint closure** — Hormuz AND Red Sea simultaneously. This is without historical precedent. The market is pricing in a short war (S&P only -4.2%), but if the conflict extends beyond April, the oil supply shock becomes an inflation shock becomes a growth shock. The IEA's 400M barrel reserve release buys approximately 20 days at the disruption level. After that, if Hormuz remains closed, we enter uncharted territory.

### For Historical Precedent Analysis:
The closest analogues are:
1. **1981 Osirak strike** (Israeli strike on Iraqi nuclear reactor) — but that was a single surgical strike, not a sustained campaign
2. **2003 Iraq invasion** — similar in scope but with ground troops; regime fell in 3 weeks
3. **1973 Arab oil embargo** — for the economic/energy dimension
4. **1979 Iranian Revolution** — for the regime change/succession dimension
5. **June 2025 Israel-Iran war** — the immediate predecessor, which was a more limited 12-day campaign that degraded nuclear facilities but failed to resolve the underlying issues

## 5. SOURCES OF UNCERTAINTY — What Would Materially Change This Analysis

1. **Ceasefire developments:** Any credible ceasefire talks would dramatically change the outlook. As of March 16, both sides have rejected mediation.
2. **Ground invasion announcement:** If the US/Israel announces ground operations inside Iran, this becomes a fundamentally different conflict.
3. **Iranian nuclear breakout:** If Iran announces it has assembled a nuclear device from its remaining enriched material, this changes everything.
4. **Hormuz reopening timeline:** Whether the US can organize a naval escort coalition to reopen the strait is the single most important near-term variable.
5. **Iranian regime internal dynamics:** Any coup, visible regime fracture, or popular uprising would alter trajectory.
6. **Gulf state entry into combat:** If Saudi Arabia, UAE, or others begin offensive operations against Iran (as opposed to defensive interception), the war widens dramatically.
7. **Russia/China intervention:** Any material military support (not just verbal) from Moscow or Beijing would change the strategic calculus.
8. **US domestic politics:** Congressional authorization votes, protest movements, or political backlash from US casualties could constrain the campaign.
