## 3. Key Findings

- **The war began February 28, 2026.** The US (Operation Epic Fury) and Israel (Operation Roaring Lion) launched the largest combined air campaign since the 2003 Iraq War, striking over 6,000 targets in Iran's first 17 days. Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei was killed on Day 1. This followed a prior, smaller US–Israeli nuclear strike campaign in June 2025 (Operation Midnight Hammer) that failed to resolve the underlying threat.

- **Iran's conventional military has been largely destroyed — but the regime has not collapsed.** US officials claim Iran's ballistic missile launch rate is down ~90%, its navy has been sunk or disabled, and its air force and air defenses have been effectively eliminated. However, Iran's new Supreme Leader, **Mojtaba Khamenei** (son of the late leader, named March 8), issued a defiant first statement on March 12 pledging continued resistance. His physical condition is disputed; he has not appeared publicly on video. The IRGC is effectively running the war.

- **The nuclear threat is degraded, not eliminated.** Both the June 2025 and February 2026 strikes severely damaged Fordow, Natanz, and Isfahan. However, the IAEA confirmed it cannot locate Iran's remaining stockpile of approximately 400+ kg of 60%-enriched uranium — enough, if further processed, for multiple weapons. At least one undisclosed underground facility may remain intact.

- **The Strait of Hormuz is functionally closed.** Iran declared closure on March 2. Tanker traffic has dropped ~90–97%. The IEA released its largest-ever emergency stockpile (400 million barrels) — a measure the IEA's own Director called "not a lasting solution." Gulf producers, including the UAE, have cut output by more than half because export storage is full and no tankers are loading. The IEA estimates **~20 million barrels per day** of crude and product flows are currently disrupted — the largest supply disruption in the history of the global oil market.

- **Diplomacy is completely frozen.** Iran's Foreign Minister stated on March 15: *"We never asked for a ceasefire and we have never asked even for negotiation."* Trump declared he is "not ready to seek a deal." Mediation efforts by Oman, Egypt, Turkey, and Pakistan have all stalled. A parallel Israeli–Lebanese direct negotiation process is being assembled (Paris or Cyprus), but this addresses only the secondary front.

- **Regional contagion is accelerating.** Iran has struck nine countries with missiles and drones (UAE, Saudi Arabia, Qatar, Kuwait, Bahrain, Oman, Jordan, Iraq, Cyprus). Hezbollah opened a second front from Lebanon on March 2. Israel began ground operations in southern Lebanon. An estimated 850+ people have been killed in Lebanon alone, with nearly one million displaced.

- **A senior US counterterrorism official resigned on March 17**, publicly citing the war and blaming Israel — the first significant internal dissent in the Trump administration over the conflict.

## 4. Scenario Outlook

The research team developed three forward scenarios:

**Scenario A — Short War / Ceasefire Within 4–6 Weeks (Probability: ~25%):** Iran accepts a negotiated pause under sustained military pressure; Hormuz reopens under US naval escort. Oil falls back to $70–80/bbl. This is what equity markets are currently pricing — and it requires a dramatic reversal by at least one party, which current evidence does not support.

**Scenario B — Protracted War of Attrition, 2–6 Months (Probability: ~40–45%):** The air campaign continues at reduced intensity; Hormuz remains partly disrupted with high-risk transit; back-channel diplomacy eventually produces an off-ramp. Oil averages $90–$120/bbl through mid-2026. This is the team's **base case**.

**Scenario C — Major Regional Escalation (Probability: ~25%):** Ground operations expand; Houthis fully re-enter (they have signaled readiness but are holding back); Iraqi militias escalate against US bases; Iran deploys mines in the Strait; oil reaches $130–$200/bbl; global recession risk rises sharply. Key trigger indicators to watch: Houthi Red Sea attacks resuming at scale, Iranian mine-laying confirmed, and/or significant US naval casualties.
