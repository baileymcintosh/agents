## Situation Overview

### Origins: Two Wars in Nine Months

The current conflict cannot be understood without its direct precursor. On **June 21–22, 2025**, the United States launched **Operation Midnight Hammer** — the largest B-2 strategic bomber operation in US history. Seven B-2 Spirit stealth bombers flying 30-hour round-trip missions from Whiteman Air Force Base, Missouri, dropped **14 GBU-57 Massive Ordnance Penetrator (MOP) bunker-busting bombs** on Iran's nuclear facilities at Fordow and Natanz, while a guided-missile submarine simultaneously launched more than two dozen Tomahawk cruise missiles against Isfahan. More than 125 aircraft and 75 precision-guided weapons were employed in the strike, which Joint Chiefs Chairman General Dan Caine called "extremely severe damage and destruction" and Trump declared had "completely and totally obliterated" Iran's nuclear program.

That declaration proved premature. Independent assessments — the IAEA, IISS, Arms Control Association, and Carnegie Endowment — concluded the strikes were "disruptive" but **did not eliminate the nuclear program.** Iran retained approximately **400–460 kilograms of 60%-enriched uranium** (theoretically sufficient for ~10 weapons if further enriched), expelled all IAEA inspectors on July 2, 2025, and spent the subsequent eight months dispersing its nuclear material, hardening underground facilities, rebuilding decentralized missile launch capabilities, and preparing the Strait of Hormuz for closure operations. The Carnegie Endowment warned at the time that Operation Midnight Hammer would likely prove "a temporary pause — delaying rather than preventing the next confrontation."

That prediction proved correct. Between June 2025 and February 2026, five rounds of indirect US-Iran nuclear talks mediated by Oman produced no breakthrough. The fundamental impasse: Washington demanded zero enrichment; Tehran insisted on its sovereign right to enrich. By February 2026, US intelligence reported Iran was actively reconstituting nuclear activities at Natanz and Fordow under concealment. Diplomatic talks collapsed on approximately February 17–27, 2026.

### The Trigger and Opening Campaign

On **February 28, 2026**, at 1:15 AM ET (9:45 AM Tehran time — the first day of Ramadan — a deliberate psychological choice), the United States and Israel launched a simultaneous combined strike campaign:

- **Operation Epic Fury** (US, CENTCOM, Admiral Brad Cooper commanding)
- **Operation Roaring Lion** (IDF, under PM Netanyahu)

The opening 24 hours saw more than **1,000 targets struck** — making it the most concentrated joint air campaign since Operation Desert Storm (1991). Israel deployed approximately **200 fighter jets** striking ~500 targets; US assets included F/A-18 Super Hornets and F-35s from carrier groups, Tomahawk salvos from submarines and surface ships, and B-2 and B-1 bombers flying from the continental United States on 37-hour sorties.

The most consequential act of the opening hours: **Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei was killed at his compound on Pasteur Street, Tehran.** The NSA had tracked Khamenei for months; US and Israeli intelligence had identified a simultaneous gathering of senior political and military leadership at the compound, enabling a mass-decapitation strike. Also killed: IRGC Commander Mohammed Pakpour, Chief of Staff Ali Asghar Hejazi, nuclear weaponization chief Reza Mozafari-Nia, former President Mahmoud Ahmadinejad, and at least 40+ other senior officials. **Mojtaba Khamenei — the Supreme Leader's 56-year-old son — lost his father, mother, wife, and one son in the strikes.**

A catastrophic early error: a missile struck **the Shajareh Tayyebeh girls' elementary school in Minab**, near Bandar Abbas, killing approximately 148–180 people, predominantly schoolchildren and teachers. This single incident has become the most politically damaging event of the campaign for the US-Israeli coalition, generating Congressional scrutiny and widespread international condemnation.

### The Strait of Hormuz: History's Largest Oil Supply Disruption

The IRGC formally declared the Strait of Hormuz closed on **March 2–4, 2026.** This is the first-ever closure of the world's most critical oil chokepoint by Iran — through which approximately **20 million barrels per day (mb/d)** of crude oil and LNG ordinarily transit, representing ~20% of global seaborne oil supply.

**The closure is primarily a commercial, not a physical, blockade.** The US Fifth Fleet commands the strait militarily and Iran cannot permanently mine a 21-mile-wide waterway against the world's most powerful navy. What has happened is a **commercial shipping shutdown driven by risk and insurance**:
- All major carriers — Maersk, MSC, CMA CGM, Hapag-Lloyd — suspended transits by early March
- P&I insurance for tankers cancelled effective March 5
- ~150 tankers anchored on both sides; tanker traffic down to ~2 vessels/day (from 138/day pre-conflict, per UKMTO/Windward AI)
- Shipping diversions surged 360% to over 1,000/day
- Gulf producers — UAE, Kuwait, Iraq, Saudi Arabia, Bahrain — cut combined oil output by at least **10 mb/d** as export storage filled with nowhere to ship

The IEA declared on March 11 the **largest coordinated emergency reserve release in its history: 400 million barrels** from member strategic petroleum reserves. The IEA simultaneously declared this is **"the largest supply disruption in the history of the global oil market."** At ~10 mb/d disruption, that SPR release covers approximately **40 days** — not a permanent solution.

**Iran is selectively allowing passage** for India, Turkey, and Iraq for political reasons, creating an asymmetric situation: Iran continues its own "shadow fleet" exports (~1.5 mb/d) while blocking all US- or Israel-affiliated shipping.

A false alarm on March 12 when US Energy Secretary Chris Wright incorrectly posted that the "first tanker had been escorted through the Strait" (quickly deleted) sent markets sharply higher before correction — illustrating how sensitive traders are to any Hormuz reopening signal. Analysts at the Center for Naval Analyses warn that US naval escort operations remain premature because the confined geography leaves vessels "dangerously exposed to land-based Iranian threats."

**Iran is not going away from this.** New Supreme Leader Mojtaba Khamenei's first statement (March 12, read by a TV anchor): *"The lever of blocking the Strait of Hormuz must definitely continue to be used."* Iranian Parliamentary Speaker Ghalibaf: *"We are absolutely NOT seeking a ceasefire.
