## 5. Risks to Research Quality

### Critical Risks

1. **Information Fog of War**: This is an active, ongoing conflict as of March 16, 2026. Casualty figures, damage assessments, and strategic intentions are all contested. The Builder must clearly label the confidence level of each claim.

2. **Source Bias**: ISW/CTP tend toward US/Israeli framing; Al Jazeera and Iranian sources toward Tehran's perspective. The report must triangulate across multiple source ecosystems.

3. **Rapidly Evolving Situation**: By the time this report is complete, significant new developments may have occurred (e.g., Lebanon ground invasion, ceasefire breakthrough, further escalation). The report should be structured to accommodate updates.

4. **Nuclear Uncertainty is the Biggest Analytical Risk**: The status of Iran's 400+ kg of 60% enriched uranium and the Pickaxe Mountain facility are the most consequential unknowns. If Iran retains enough material for a breakout, the entire strategic calculus changes. Multiple expert sources (ISIS, Arms Control Association, FDD) disagree on the effectiveness of strikes.

5. **Mission Creep Analysis**: The Al Jazeera piece on mission creep is analytically important — the conflict appears to be following the historical pattern of expanding objectives (from nuclear/missile elimination → regime disruption → internal security degradation → democratic aspirations). This pattern must be analyzed honestly, even if uncomfortable.

6. **Market Data Specificity**: Financial data points are scattered across multiple sources with different timestamps. The Builder should collect and organize: oil price trajectory (from $61 pre-war to $100+ now), gold (~$5,000-5,400), S&P 500 moves, DXY, and 10Y yields — with dates — for use in later cycles.

### Mitigation
- Every factual claim tagged with source and date
- Explicit "confidence level" markers (confirmed/probable/possible/uncertain)
- Modular report structure that allows updates without rewriting
- Cross-reference Western, regional, and Iranian sources for key claims
