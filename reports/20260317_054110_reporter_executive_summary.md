Three credible endgame scenarios have emerged from open-source analysis (CNN/McGurk, Guardian, TIAA CIO, ifo Institute):

High-intensity strikes end. Iran's political structure survives in weakened form. Heavy sanctions remain. The Strait gradually reopens. Oil prices fall back toward $70–$80/barrel by Q3–Q4 2026. **Scenario 2 — Prolonged Attrition / Regional Escalation (Probability: ~25–30%)**
Iran successfully sustains Hormuz closure for months by mining the waterway and attacking naval assets. Oil stays above $100/barrel. Gulf states face prolonged instability. A negotiated end takes 3–6+ months, with lasting structural damage to global supply chains, stagflation risk in Europe and Asia, and weakened U.S. credibility.

**Scenario 3 — Regime Collapse and Chaos (Probability: ~10–15%)**
Sustained strikes and internal pressure cause the Iranian regime to fracture. The IRGC splinters; the new Supreme Leader — reportedly wounded and lacking his father's legitimacy — loses command authority. However, this scenario does **not** guarantee a better outcome: regime collapse could mean a power vacuum, ethnic fragmentation, and a dangerous scramble for Iran's estimated 440kg of highly enriched uranium. Most analysts consider this the least likely but most dangerous path.
