## Situation Overview

On **February 28, 2026, at 1:15 AM Eastern Time** — the first day of Ramadan — the United States and Israel simultaneously launched **Operation Epic Fury** (US/CENTCOM) and **Operation Roaring Lion** (IDF), the most concentrated joint air campaign since Operation Desert Storm in 1991. In the first 24 hours alone, more than **900 US strikes** and **200 Israeli fighter jets striking ~500 targets** were executed in a single synchronized wave.

This was not a spontaneous escalation. It was the second major US-Israeli military operation against Iran in less than nine months. The first, **Operation Midnight Hammer** (June 21–22, 2025 US time), struck Iran's declared nuclear facilities at **Fordow, Natanz, and Isfahan** with GBU-57 Massive Ordnance Penetrators dropped by seven B-2 Spirit stealth bombers. President Trump declared the program "completely and totally obliterated." It was not. Iran spent the following eight months dispersing its enriched uranium stockpile, rebuilding missile infrastructure, and hardening tunnel sites — a textbook "Osirak effect" in which a partially successful strike accelerates covert reconstitution.

Three rounds of Oman-mediated nuclear talks (February 6, 17, and 26, 2026), led on the US side by Steve Witkoff and Jared Kushner and on the Iranian side by FM Abbas Araghchi, collapsed on approximately **February 26–27**. The US demanded full dismantlement of enrichment capacity; Iran offered stockpile reduction but refused to surrender its enrichment right. Trump signed the execution order for Epic Fury on February 27 while aboard Air Force One — paradoxically, **one day after Iran had reportedly agreed to reduce stockpiles "to the lowest level possible,"** a fact whose interpretation remains fiercely contested.

The conflict's stated US objectives, as articulated across multiple official statements, span three nested levels of ambition:
1. **Nuclear disarmament** — eliminating Iran's enrichment program permanently
2. **Military degradation** — destroying missile production, proxy supply chains, and naval capacity
3. **Implicit regime change** — systematic strikes on IRGC internal security apparatus and Netanyahu's explicit statements about "allowing the Iranian people to remove the tyrannical regime"

These goals are, as multiple analysts have observed, in fundamental tension with one another: an Iran fighting for regime survival has every incentive to fight rather than negotiate, making a diplomatic off-ramp structurally harder to construct with each passing day of bombing.
