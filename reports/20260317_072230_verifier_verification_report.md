## Specific Findings

### FINDING 1 — Operation Midnight Hammer Date Is Inconsistent *(Medium Severity)*
**Issue:** The report states Operation Midnight Hammer occurred on **"June 22, 2025"** in its introduction (Section 1.1, first paragraph). However, multiple authoritative sources — CSIS, Breaking Defense, the CRS Congressional Insight, and the Defense Threat Reduction Agency — uniformly date the strike to the evening of **June 21, 2025 (US time)**, with the Pentagon briefing occurring on June 22. The report cites June 22 in one place and June 21 implicitly elsewhere. The correct date is June 21, 2025 (evening EST / June 22 Tehran time).

**Severity:** Medium. The date confusion is minor in isolation but undermines confidence in the precision of other chronological claims, and in an intelligence product, timelines matter.

**Recommended Fix:** Standardize to June 21, 2025 (US/Eastern time), noting June 22 as the local Tehran impact date and the date of the Pentagon briefing.

### FINDING 2 — SPND Chairman Identification Discrepancy *(High Severity)*
**Issue:** The report identifies the SPND (Iran's nuclear weaponization organization) Chairman killed on February 28 as **"Reza Mozafari-Nia."** However, the Axios confirmed-kills list — one of the most authoritative early casualty compilations, sourced to Israeli officials — identifies the SPND Chairman killed as **"Hossein Jabal Amelian,"** describing Mozafari-Nia as the *former* SPND Chairman. The JINSA March 1 update (which the report cites) lists him as "Former SPND Chairman Reza Mozaffari-Nia," meaning both were killed, but their roles are conflated in the report.

**Severity:** High. In a decapitation-strike assessment, precision about *which official held which position* at time of death is analytically critical — it affects the assessment of institutional continuity in Iran's weapons program. Describing a former chairman as the current chairman misrepresents the operational impact of the strike.

**Recommended Fix:** Clarify that **Hossein Jabal Amelian** was the incumbent SPND Chairman at the time of the strike; Mozafari-Nia was the former Chairman. Note both were killed, but attribute the current leadership position accurately.

### FINDING 5 — Strait of Hormuz Characterization Understates De Facto Closure *(Medium Severity)*
**Issue:** Key Finding #3 characterizes the Strait as a **"contested, high-risk corridor, not a closed chokepoint"** and emphasizes Iran's UN Ambassador's statement that Iran is "not going to close the Strait." However, verified sources — including the Wikipedia 2026 Strait of Hormuz Crisis article, Reuters, Lloyds List, Container Management, and WC Shipping — describe the strait as **"effectively closed"** to commercial traffic, with tanker traffic dropping to near zero by March 3. The IRGC confirmed the closure. QatarEnergy declared force majeure on all LNG shipments. Insurance protections were cancelled effective March 5. Over 150 tankers anchored outside the strait.

The report's characterization is technically defensible (the US Navy has not been physically blocked) but operationally misleading. The distinction between "physically closed" and "commercially non-navigable" is not adequately explained, which risks misrepresenting the energy supply crisis to executive readers.

**Severity:** Medium. Analytically clever, but practically insufficient for a decision-support document.

**Recommended Fix:** Retain the technical distinction but add clear language that from a commercial and energy-supply perspective, the strait's effective closure is functionally equivalent to a physical blockade for most purposes. The UN Ambassador's statement should not be used to minimize a crisis that multiple sources describe as "the largest supply disruption in the history of the global oil market" (IEA, March 2026).
