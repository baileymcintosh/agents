# Evolving Our Equities Franchise

November 06, 2025

![Image 1](https://www.citadelsecurities.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2025/07/Web-JimEsposito-Banner-1024x291.png)

Dear Clients,

We’re excited to announce the launch of Citadel Securities’ High Touch Equities business – built the Citadel Securities way: through technology, partnership, and innovation. We’ve recently welcomed Elan Luger to lead this initiative and are assembling a world-class team around him. Elan and I will soon be meeting with many of you to share how we’re designing this business to deliver the execution quality, transparency, and insight you expect from Citadel Securities.

With our leading position in U.S. equities – trading nearly 25% of the daily volume – along with our operational scale and unique approach to risk management, we’re well-positioned to offer exceptional pricing and execution.

But we’re not building a business that competes solely on price – we’re building one designed to meet your evolving execution needs and help shape the markets of tomorrow.

We are also developing a suite of data and analytics tools that leverage our unique insights into global equity flows to help you make superior trading decisions.

And we’re just getting started. We’ll continue to iterate, innovate, and expand.

We’re currently beta testing our High Touch Equities platform ahead of a Q1 formal launch and welcome your feedback as we refine our offering. If you’d like to learn more, please reach out to me or Elan directly. Our goal remains the same: to understand the challenges you face and help solve them through both market expertise and better analytics. I’m confident Citadel Securities is uniquely positioned to help you navigate – and shape – the capital markets of the future.

We look forward to continuing our partnership.

## Fall Reads, Watches, and Listens

### 🎧 Dwarkesh: Andrej Karpathy – AGI is Still a Decade Away

“Andrej Karpathy, a widely admired pioneer of deep learning, provides a balanced take on AI in 2025. He celebrates the progress but points out that current coding models aren’t yet useful on novel tasks, and many problems remain unsolved.”

Vahe Musoyan Managing Director, Quantitative Research

### 📖Breakneck: China’s Quest to Engineer the Future

“Dan Wang speaks of China as an engineering state utilizing centralized control to keep building infrastructure, whilst the US has become dominated by lawyers keen to enact regulation…both good and bad. The book provides an illuminating discussion of the pros and cons of China’s approach, whilst highlighting the risks of wayward American leadership, the price of which could be losing the technological arms race. Fascinating and topical stuff!”

Nohshad Shah Head of Fixed Income Sales, EMEA

### 📰Nonconsensus: The AI Boom’s Real Economy Problem

“Bob Elliott turns a macro investor’s lens onto tech fundamentals. Noting that Meta is not seeing an acceleration in revenue that matches the scale of its capex, he presents a compelling argument that the real economy’s capacity to pay might be the limiting factor for returns on AI investments.”

Ryan Preclaw Head of Content Data Science

### 🎧A.I. School Is in Session: Two Takes on the Future of Education

“This episode of Hard Fork is a very good, practical discussion on how to think about AI in secondary and post-secondary education. Graham Burnett really nails the second half on the downfall of long-form content. The quotes from students at the end are also eye opening – it’s not all just kids wanting to cheat on their essays.”

Dane Skillrud COO, Systematic FICC and Equities

### 📺WSJ: I Tried the First Humanoid Home Robot. It Got Weird.

“The Neo robot’s unveiling has created quite a stir. On the one hand, Neo represents a clear path to robots living among us as household helpers for $500/month in a year. On the other, Neo will rely on remote human operators at launch. What is autonomy? How much privacy are we willing to sacrifice for automation?”

Jeff Maurone COO, Technology and Low Latency

### 📺PBS: The American Revolution

“Thanks, in part, to generous support from Ken Griffin, America’s master storyteller, Ken Burns, unveils his latest six-part epic on PBS starting on November 16. The creative force behind “Baseball,” “Ernest Hemingway” and “The Civil War” turns his lens on the country’s tenuous and ultimately triumphant origin story.”

Josh King Head of Corporate Affairs
