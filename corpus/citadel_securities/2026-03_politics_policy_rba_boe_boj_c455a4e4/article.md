# Politics & Policy (RBA/BOE/BOJ)

January 26, 2026

### By Frank Flight

Long end JGBs have performed well since we turned constructive earlier in the week, but sustained performance will be more challenging. The UK political outlook remains a headwind to policy rate pricing that – based on the economic data – should look increasingly dovish. We continue to think the RBA is on track for a February rate hike, and market pricing is starting to reflect that following stronger jobs data.

### RBA: Increased Conviction in Our Call for a Feb Rate Hike

We highlighted in early January that we thought the case was building for a February rate hike from the RBA, despite markets pricing just 6bps. The market pricing at 15bps has now started to reflect that view following a material beat in employment data. We are increasingly convicted that the RBA will raise rates in February.
