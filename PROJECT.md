## Research Scope

### 1. Geopolitical & Military Situation (February–March 2026, ONGOING)
- Current state of the conflict as of today: what military actions have occurred, who has struck what, what is the current operational status
- Search queries to use: "Iran US Israel war 2026", "Iran military strikes March 2026", "Iran nuclear sites attacked 2026", "Middle East war 2026 latest"
- Iran's strategic position: military capabilities, proxy network (Hezbollah, Houthis, PMF, etc.), nuclear program status
- Key adversaries and their objectives: Israel, US, Gulf states, others
- Regional dynamics: Saudi Arabia, UAE, Qatar, Iraq, Syria, Turkey — their positions and interests

### 2. Historical Precedents
- Wars and conflicts involving Iran and its predecessors (Iran-Iraq War, 1979 Revolution, Gulf Wars)
- Historical cases of oil supply disruptions and their market effects (1973, 1979, 1990)
- Proxy war dynamics: historical cases and how they escalate or de-escalate
- Nuclear standoffs: how they have resolved (North Korea, Libya, South Africa, Pakistan)
- Israeli military strikes on nuclear facilities (Osirak 1981, Syria 2007) — lessons

### 3. Key Player Analysis
- **Iran**: regime ideology, decision-making structure, internal factions, economic constraints, red lines
- **Israel**: war cabinet dynamics, strategic objectives, domestic politics, intelligence posture
- **United States**: policy position, military presence in region, domestic political constraints
- **Russia & China**: interests, support for Iran, how conflict affects their calculus
- **Gulf States**: Saudi Arabia's positioning, UAE's role, their economic exposure
- **Proxy actors**: Hezbollah, Houthis, Hamas — capabilities, objectives, independence vs. Iran control

### 4. Game Theory & Strategic Analysis
- Escalation ladders: what actions trigger what responses
- Red lines for each major actor — what would cause direct military intervention
- Deterrence dynamics and where they may be breaking down
- Most likely negotiation off-ramps and what conditions make them possible
- Application of game theory frameworks (chicken, prisoner's dilemma, signaling theory)

### 5. Scenarios & Probability Estimates
Develop at least 5 distinct scenarios with estimated probabilities, timelines, and key triggers:
- Scenario A: Contained regional conflict (no major escalation)
- Scenario B: Israeli strike on Iranian nuclear facilities
- Scenario C: Iranian nuclear breakout
- Scenario D: Full regional war (multiple state actors)
- Scenario E: Diplomatic resolution / de-escalation
- [Additional scenarios as warranted by analysis]

For each scenario: probability estimate, key triggers, timeline, winners/losers, market impact

### 6. Financial Markets Impact Analysis
- **Oil & energy markets**: price scenarios under each conflict outcome, Strait of Hormuz risk
- **Safe haven flows**: gold, USD, Treasuries, CHF — historical patterns and current setup
- **Equities**: defense sector, energy sector, regional markets (Israel, Gulf), broader risk-off
- **Credit markets**: sovereign spreads in affected countries, broader EM impact
- **Second-order effects**: inflation pass-through, central bank responses, supply chain disruption
- **Historical market behavior**: how markets moved in analogous conflicts (Gulf War 1990, 2003 Iraq, etc.)
- **Positioning**: what institutional investors are likely doing, where consensus may be wrong

## Quality Standard

- No hedging for the sake of hedging — take positions, explain the reasoning
- Flag explicitly where real-time information would change the analysis
